# Korean Grammar Patterns

---

## 1. AV+게 하다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-게 하다

**Meaning**:
Make/let/have someone do something (causative)

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>게 하다
<u>AVst</u>게 했다

**Usage**:
Used to express causation - making, letting, or having someone do something. The subject causes or allows another person to perform an action. Can express both permission (let) and compulsion (make) depending on context.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(었,았,였)다]]

---

## 2. V+(ㄹ,을)지 V+(ㄹ,을)지

**Yonsei Signature:**
-ㄹ지/을지 -ㄹ지/을지

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-을지 -을지

**Meaning**:
whether to... or to... (deliberation between options)

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>(을,ㄹ)지 <u>AVst</u>(을,ㄹ)지 \_\_\_\_

**Usage**:
Expresses indecision or uncertainty between two or more mutually exclusive options. Often followed by 모르다 (don't know) or 고민하다 (worry/deliberate).

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+기]], [[V+(을,ㄹ)]], [[V+(는,은,ㄴ,을,ㄹ)지]]

## 3. V+(기는,긴) 하다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-기는 하다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-기는 하다

**Meaning**:
Partial Acknowledgment

**Grammar**:
<u>Vst</u>기는 하다.
<u>Vst</u>긴 하다.

**Usage**:
In the form 기는/긴 하다, the speaker acknowledges that something is true or is indeed the case. It is often followed by -지만 or -는데/ㄴ데 grammar to form a partial acknowledgment with an objection.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+기]], [[N+(은,는)]], [[V+기는(요)]]

## 4. V+(나,은가,ㄴ가) 보다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-나/은가/ㄴ가 보다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-나 보다

**Meaning**:
It seems like, it appears that (conjecture based on observation)

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>나 보다
(있,없)나 보다
<u>DVst</u>(은가,ㄴ가) 보다
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)나 보다
<u>N</u>인가 보다

**Usage**:
Used to express conjecture or assumption based on indirect evidence or observation. The speaker infers something from what they've seen, heard, or experienced. Less certain than direct observation with -네요, but more certain than pure speculation with -겠-.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(나,은가,ㄴ가)]], [[V+(었,았,였)다]]

## 5. V+(는,ㄴ,_)다고 생각하다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-는다고/ㄴ다고/다고생각하다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-는다고 생각하다

**Meaning**:
I think that..., I believe that...

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>(는,ㄴ)다고 생각하다
<u>DVst</u>다고 생각하다
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)다고 생각하다
<u>Vst</u>(을,ㄹ) 거라고 생각하다
<u>N</u>(이)라고 생각하다

**Usage**:
Used to express one's opinion, thought, or belief about something. Converts a statement into reported speech followed by the verb 생각하다 (to think). Commonly used to soften assertions or express personal viewpoints politely.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(는,ㄴ,_)다고 하다]]

## 6. V+(는,ㄴ,_)다면

**Yonsei Signature:**
-는다면/ㄴ다면/다면

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-는다면

**Meaning**:
If (it is the case that)..., supposing that...

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>(는,ㄴ)다면 \_\_\_\_
<u>DVst</u>다면 \_\_\_\_
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)다면 \_\_\_\_
<u>N</u>(이)라면 \_\_\_\_

**Usage**:
A conditional form that combines declarative ending -(는)다 with conditional -면. Used to present hypothetical situations or suppositions, often about factual statements or things you've heard. More formal than simple -(으)면 and often used when the condition is based on reported information or general facts.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(는,ㄴ,_)다고 하다]], [[V+(으)면]]

## 7. V+(는,ㄴ,_)다면서(요)

**Yonsei Signature:**
-는다면서요/ㄴ다면서요/다면서요

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-는다면서요

**Meaning**:
I heard that..., they say that... (seeking confirmation)

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>(는,ㄴ)다면서(요)
<u>DVst</u>다면서(요)
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)다면서(요)
<u>Vst</u>(을,ㄹ) 거라면서(요)
<u>N</u>(이)라면서(요)

**Usage**:
Used to confirm or verify information that the speaker has heard from someone else. Often carries the nuance of "Isn't it true that...?" or "I heard that... is that right?" Combines reported speech -(는)다고 with -면서 (while/even though) to create a confirmation-seeking question.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(는,ㄴ,_)다고 하다]], [[V+(으)면서]]

## 8. V+(는,ㄴ,_)대요

**Yonsei Signature:**
-는대요/ㄴ대요/대요

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-는대요

**Meaning**:
They say..., I heard that... (casual reported speech)

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>(는,ㄴ)대요
<u>DVst</u>대요
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)대요
<u>Vst</u>(을,ㄹ) 거래요
<u>N</u>(이)래요

**Usage**:
A contracted form of -(는)다고 해요, used to relay information heard from others in casual conversation. More informal than the full form and commonly used in everyday speech to share news, gossip, or secondhand information.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(는,ㄴ,_)다고 하다]]

## 9. V+(는,은,ㄴ) 편이다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-는/은/ㄴ 편이다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-는 편이다

**Meaning**:
tend to, on the {adjective} side, rather, fairly

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>는 편이다
<u>DVst</u>(은,ㄴ) 편이다
<u>AVst</u>(은,ㄴ) 편이다

**Usage**:
Used to express general tendencies or relative positioning on a spectrum. Softens absolute statements - makes them more moderate/diplomatic.

**Notes:**
- Cannot be used with extreme absolutes or binary states (e.g., death, existence)
- Action verbs need to have a quantity modifier before the verb: 자주, 정도면, 

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(는,은,ㄴ)]], [[V+(은,ㄴ)]]

## 10. V+(는,은,ㄴ)데도

**Yonsei Signature:**
-는데도/은데도/ㄴ데도

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-는데도

**Meaning**:
Even though, despite the fact that

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>는데도 \_\_\_\_
(있,없)는데도 \_\_\_\_
<u>DVst</u>(은,ㄴ)데도 \_\_\_\_
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)데도 \_\_\_\_

**Usage**:
Expresses contrast or unexpectedness - despite the situation in the first clause, something contrary happens in the second clause. Stronger than simple -는데 as it emphasizes that the second clause is surprising or contrary to expectations. Combines -(는)데 with 도 (also/even).

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(는,은,ㄴ)데]], [[N+도]]

## 11. V+(어,아,여) 놓다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-어/아/여 놓다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-어 놓다

do and leave (in that state), do in advance (for later use)

**Grammar**:
<u>N</u>(을,를) <u>AVst</u>(어,아,여) 놓다.

**Related Patterns:**

## 13. V+(어,아,여) 있다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-어/아/여 있다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-어 있다

**Meaning**:
Continuing or resultant state

**Grammar**:
<u>N</u>(이,가) <u>AVst</u>(어,아,여) 있다.

**Usage**:
Emphasizes the continuing state resulting from an action rather than the action itself.

**Notes:**
- Only works with certain action verbs that can result in a maintained state
- Compatible verbs: 앉다, 서다, 입다, 쓰다, 신다, 놓다, 걸다, etc.
- NOT compatible with momentary or consumption verbs: *먹어 있다, *가 있다

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(어,아,여)]], [[V+(어,아,여) 두다]], [[V+(어,아,여) 놓다]]

## 14. V+(어,아,여)서 그런지

**Yonsei Signature:**
-어서/아서/여서 그런지

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-어서 그런지

**Meaning**:
Perhaps because..., maybe it's because...

**Grammar**:
<u>Vst</u>(어,아,여)서 그런지 \_\_\_\_
<u>N</u>(어,아,여)서 그런지 \_\_\_\_

**Usage**:
Used to suggest a possible reason or cause for something, with some uncertainty. The speaker is speculating that the situation in the first clause might be the reason for the result in the second clause. More tentative than simple -어서, as 그런지 adds the nuance of "I'm not entirely sure, but maybe..."

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(어,아,여)서]]

## 15. V+(어,아,여)야지(요)

**Yonsei Signature:**
-어야지요/아야지요/여야지요

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-어야지요

**Meaning**:
should, ought to (casual agreement/acknowledgment)

**Grammar**:
<u>Vst</u>(어,아,여)야지요.
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)어야지요.
<u>N</u>(이)어야지요.

**Usage**:
More casual and conversational than -(어,아,여)야 하다/되다. Often expresses agreement with an obvious truth or acknowledgment. More gentle than -(어,아,여)야 돼 (must/have to).

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(어,아,여)]], [[V+(어,아,여)야]], [[V+지(요)]], [[V+(었,았,였)다]]

## 16. V+(었,았,였)더니

**Yonsei Signature:**
-었더니/았더니/였더니

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-었더니

**Meaning**:
After doing..., when I/someone did... (then discovered)

**Grammar**:
<u>AVst</u>(었,았,였)더니 \_\_\_\_

**Usage**:
Indicates that after completing the action in the first clause, the speaker discovered or experienced the result in the second clause. Often expresses surprise, discovery, or an unexpected outcome. The subject of the first clause is typically the speaker (first person) when used with action verbs.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(었,았,였)다]],

## 17. V+(었,았,였)을 것이다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-었을/았을/였을 것이다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-었을 것이다

**Meaning**:
Would have, must have (past conjecture/counterfactual)

**Grammar**:
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)을 것이다

**Usage**:
Used to make conjectures about past events or to express counterfactual situations (what would have happened). Can indicate: (1) supposition about what probably happened in the past, or (2) hypothetical outcomes in past conditional contexts. Combines past tense with the future modifier and 것이다 to create past speculation or counterfactual meaning.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(었,았,였)다]], [[V+(을,ㄹ) 것이다]]

## 21. V+(으)면서도

**Yonsei Signature:**
-으면서도/면서도

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-으면서도

**Meaning**:
While/even though, despite doing (simultaneous contrast)

**Grammar**:
<u>Vst</u>(으)면서도 \_\_\_\_
<u>Vst</u>(었,았,였)으면서도 \_\_\_\_
<u>N</u>(이)면서도 \_\_\_\_

**Usage**:
Expresses that despite the situation or action in the first clause (which might normally prevent or contradict the second), the second clause still occurs. Combines -(으)면서 (while) with 도 (even/also) to create a concessive meaning stronger than simple -(으)면서. Often used to express contradiction or unexpected simultaneous states.

**Notes:**

**Related Patterns:**
[[V+(으)면서]], [[N+도]]

## 23. V+(을,ㄹ) 뿐이다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-을/ㄹ 뿐이다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**

## 24. V+(을,ㄹ)지도 모르다

**Yonsei Signature:**
-을지도/ㄹ지도 모르다

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-을지도 모르다

## 35. V+(는,ㄴ)다는 것이

**Yonsei Signature:**
-는다는/ㄴ다는 것이

**Yonsei Signature Short:**
-는다는 것이

**Meaning:**
I meant to... but (intention vs. result)

**Grammar:**
<u>AVst</u>(는,ㄴ)다는 것이 \_\_\_\_ (result/mistake)

**Usage:**
Used when the speaker's original intention or plan resulted in a different (often negative or mistaken) outcome. The clause usually ends with a description of the mistake or unintended action.
